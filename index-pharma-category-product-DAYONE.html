Overview

The food pyramid is an easy guideline used to control the number of different types of food according to the daily meal recommendations. The pyramid was first introduced in Sweden in 1974. The Swedish at that time revolted because of the unreasonably high price of food. The Swedish government then set up a committee to analyze the possibility of combining a balanced diet with all the nutrients at a low cost. The slogan to promote this food pyramid is ‘A good, healthy diet at a reasonable price’. Initially, the pyramid contained only three levels and then was modified to five levels in 2008 by the Harvard School of Public Health which includes more than just food to make a comprehensive healthy lifestyle guide. This food pyramid will be explained in more detail in the section of food pyramid levels.

Later on, the pyramid was adapted in 1992 by the United States under the Department of Agriculture and was named the ‘Food Guide Pyramid’. The objective is to give recommendations of foods in sufficient quantities and moderate portions. Other countries are also interested in the concept of visual representation for nutritional recommendations including Japan, Denmark and Sri Lanka. These countries created their own nutritional guides such as in the form of flags, pagodas, rainbows and wheels.

Quicktips

Carbohydrates are the main source of human energy and they are needed to perform daily activities. In this case, the selection of unprocessed carbohydrate foods is the main and better choice for our health because it contains more nutrients such as fiber, vitamins and minerals compared to processed carbohydrate foods. Examples of the unprocessed foods which are rich in carbohydrates are:

  • Cereals
  • Brown rice
  • Oats
  • Whole Grain products like bread, noodles and pasta

Mixed Dosage

Protein is an important source of energy after carbohydrates. Some of the main functions of protein are used for the growth and formation of tissues, muscles, blood, enzymes, bones, skins and also the body's resistance. You are encouraged to consume low-fat protein sources such as chicken breast or other muscular pieces of meat. In order to get proteins from seafood, you are encouraged to consume fishes that are rich in omega-3 such as salmon, sardines and mackerel.

For vegetarians, There are alternatives to protein other than meat for vegetarians. It can be found in some plants and nuts. Some of the plants or nutty products that are rich in protein are tofu and tempeh.

Frequently Asked Questions

Fiber is the third component in the Malaysian Healthy Plate and this component is emphasized in the concept of Healthy Plate because there are many Malaysians who ignore the intake of vegetables and fruits in their diet. The advantages of fiber not only provide low energy, iron, minerals and vitamins, but it can also speed up bowel movements and avoid the risk of getting constipation.

If you are cooking at home, reduce the use of oil or butter in your cooking as it can increase the risk of developing hypercholesterolemia and hypertension. If you eat outside such as in a restaurant or stall, choose foods that are using oil in small quantities or ask the cook to reduce the oil for the menu that you have selected. You are also encouraged to eat home-cooked food as it is cleaner and healthier and you can also control the content of all your cooking ingredients.

Besides, the intake of sweet drinks should also be reduced or avoided and drink plain water instead, as it contains zero calories.

In conclusion, the food pyramid is just a nutritional guide and it is up to an individual if they want to practice what has been outlined in the food pyramid and the Healthy Plate. Good nutrition is a balanced diet with a moderate portion.
